**BULL CONVICTION: HIGH**

### Bear Analyst

The bull is painting a perfect picture, but let's stress-test it.

Yes, the fundamentals look impressive — but a P/B of 30.7x and trailing P/E of 40.5x mean any execution stumble gets punished severely. That "attractive" forward P/E of 17.7x assumes the current hyper-growth trajectory holds. It won't forever.

On the AI supercycle: Big Tech capex surges historically overshoot, then correct. We've seen this movie before. And with crude near $111/bbl and the 10-year yield approaching 4.44% with near-zero rate cut odds, the macro backdrop actively compresses high-multiple valuations. Capital gets more expensive — that matters for growth stocks.

The technical picture contradicts the bull's "entry opportunity" framing. Price is trading below EMA10 ($202.99), MACD histogram has turned negative, and we're 8% off recent highs with momentum deteriorating. That's early-stage distribution, not accumulation.

Those 57 analyst price targets? That's consensus — and consensus is already priced in. When everyone's bullish, who's left to buy?

**BEAR CONVICTION: MEDIUM**
